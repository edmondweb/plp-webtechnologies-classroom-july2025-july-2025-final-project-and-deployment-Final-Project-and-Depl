About Kerniel Foundation

Founded with a deep passion for animal welfare, Kerniel Foundation emerged from a simple yet powerful belief: every dog deserves a chance at a loving, safe home. Our journey began when our founders witnessed the overwhelming number of abandoned and mistreated dogs in our community and decided to take action.

What started as a small grassroots effort has grown into a comprehensive foundation that addresses every aspect of dog rescue and rehabilitation. From emergency rescues to long-term behavioral therapy, from community education to policy advocacy, we work tirelessly to create a world where no dog is left behind.

Our Mission Statement

Our mission is to provide shelter, rehabilitation, and rehoming for abandoned dogs while promoting responsible pet ownership and a compassionate society.

Our Vision Statement

Our vision is to create a world where every dog has a loving home, a safe refuge, and a compassionate community.

Our Core Values

Compassion

We approach every rescue with empathy and understanding, recognizing that each dog has unique needs and deserves individualized care and attention.

Integrity

We operate with complete transparency and honesty in all our dealings, ensuring that every donation and effort is directed towards our mission.

Accountability

We take full responsibility for the dogs in our care and are committed to providing regular updates and maintaining the highest standards of animal welfare.

Education

We believe that education is key to preventing abandonment and promoting responsible pet ownership throughout our community.

Our Programs

Adoption Program

Our adoption program is designed to create perfect matches between dogs and families. We believe that every successful adoption starts with understanding the needs of both the dog and the adoptive family.

Step 1: Application

Complete our comprehensive adoption application including your living situation, experience with pets, and preferences.

Step 2: Interview

Meet with our adoption counselors to discuss your application and find the best match for your family.

Step 3: Meet & Greet

Spend time with your potential new companion in a comfortable, supervised environment.

Step 4: Home Visit

Our team conducts a friendly home visit to ensure a safe environment for your new pet.

Step 5: Adoption

Complete the adoption paperwork and welcome your new family member home!

Step 6: Follow-up

Receive ongoing support and resources to ensure a successful transition and lifelong bond.

Buddy's Amazing Transformation

From the brink of death to a life full of love

The Rescue: Buddy was found collapsed on a busy highway, severely malnourished and covered in infected wounds. Weighing only 25 pounds when he should have been 60, veterinarians weren't sure if he would survive the night.

The Rehabilitation: After three months of intensive medical care, proper nutrition, and gentle rehabilitation, Buddy slowly regained his strength. Our behavioral team worked patiently to help him overcome his fear of humans and learn to trust again.

The Happy Ending: Today, Buddy is thriving with the Martinez family. "He's our gentle giant," says Maria Martinez. "Buddy has brought so much joy to our home. He loves playing with our children and has become the perfect family companion. It's hard to believe this confident, loving dog was once so afraid and alone."

Follow-up: One year later, Buddy has gained healthy weight, loves going on family hiking trips, and has even learned to swim at the local lake!

🐶

Luna's Journey to Trust

Overcoming trauma through patience and love

The Rescue: Luna came to us as a victim of severe neglect and abuse. She was so traumatized that she wouldn't let anyone touch her and spent her first weeks hiding in the corner of her kennel, shaking whenever humans approached.

The Rehabilitation: Luna's recovery required a specialized approach. Our team of behavioral specialists spent months building trust through patient, consistent care. They used positive reinforcement techniques and gradually helped her learn that not all humans would hurt her.

The Happy Ending: The breakthrough came when Luna was placed with experienced foster parents who understood trauma recovery. "The first time Luna wagged her tail, we both cried," recalls foster mom Sarah Chen. The Chens eventually adopted Luna permanently. "She's transformed from a fearful, withdrawn dog into a confident, playful companion who brings light to every room she enters."

Follow-up: Luna now works as a therapy dog, helping other rescued animals learn to trust humans again. Her gentle nature and patient demeanor make her perfect for this role.

🦮

Max's Second Chance

An older dog finds new purpose

The Rescue: Max, a 9-year-old German Shepherd, was surrendered to us when his elderly owner could no longer care for him. At his age, many people assumed he would be difficult to place, but we believe every dog deserves a chance regardless of age.

The Rehabilitation: Max was already well-trained and gentle, but he struggled with separation anxiety after losing his longtime companion. Our team worked with him on confidence-building exercises and helped him adjust to the shelter environment.

The Happy Ending: Max found his perfect match with retired couple Robert and Linda Thompson. "We specifically wanted an older dog," says Robert. "Max has been the perfect companion for our daily walks and quiet evenings at home. His calm, gentle nature fits perfectly with our lifestyle." Max has also become a beloved visitor at the local nursing home, bringing comfort to residents.

Follow-up: Two years later, Max continues to thrive in his golden years, proving that older dogs make wonderful companions and deserve loving homes too.

🐕‍🦺

Bella's Brave Recovery

From injured stray to beloved family member

The Rescue: Bella was found after being hit by a car, with a severely injured hind leg and multiple fractures. The surgery to save her leg was complex and expensive, but our medical team was determined to give her the best chance at a normal life.

The Rehabilitation: Bella's recovery included months of physical therapy, pain management, and learning to walk again. Despite her challenges, she maintained a cheerful, optimistic spirit that inspired everyone who worked with her.

The Happy Ending: The Johnson family fell in love with Bella's resilient spirit. "Her determination to overcome her injuries inspired our whole family," says daughter Emma Johnson, age 12. "Bella taught us that challenges don't define you – how you face them does." Today, Bella runs and plays almost normally, with just a slight limp that doesn't slow her down at all.

Follow-up: Bella has become a symbol of resilience for our organization and often appears at fundraising events to show others the importance of never giving up on a dog in need.
