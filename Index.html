About Kerniel Foundation

Founded with a deep passion for animal welfare, Kerniel Foundation emerged from a simple yet powerful belief: every dog deserves a chance at a loving, safe home. Our journey began when our founders witnessed the overwhelming number of abandoned and mistreated dogs in our community and decided to take action.

What started as a small grassroots effort has grown into a comprehensive foundation that addresses every aspect of dog rescue and rehabilitation. From emergency rescues to long-term behavioral therapy, from community education to policy advocacy, we work tirelessly to create a world where no dog is left behind.

Our Mission Statement

Our mission is to provide shelter, rehabilitation, and rehoming for abandoned dogs while promoting responsible pet ownership and a compassionate society.

Our Vision Statement

Our vision is to create a world where every dog has a loving home, a safe refuge, and a compassionate community.

Our Core Values

Compassion

We approach every rescue with empathy and understanding, recognizing that each dog has unique needs and deserves individualized care and attention.

Integrity

We operate with complete transparency and honesty in all our dealings, ensuring that every donation and effort is directed towards our mission.

Accountability

We take full responsibility for the dogs in our care and are committed to providing regular updates and maintaining the highest standards of animal welfare.

Education

We believe that education is key to preventing abandonment and promoting responsible pet ownership throughout our community.

Our Partnerships

We work closely with local animal shelters, veterinary clinics, and corporate sponsors to maximize our impact. Our partnerships include collaborations with the City Animal Control Department, regional veterinary colleges, and numerous local businesses that support our mission through donations and volunteer programs.

Our Rescue Efforts

Emergency Response

Our 24/7 emergency response team works with local authorities to rescue dogs from dangerous situations, including natural disasters, abuse cases, and abandonment.

Network Partnerships

We collaborate with a network of shelters, rescue organizations, and volunteers to identify and rescue dogs in need across the region.

Community Reporting

Our community outreach program encourages residents to report cases of abandonment or abuse, creating a safety net for vulnerable dogs.

Rehabilitation Process

Medical Assessment

Every rescued dog receives a comprehensive medical examination, including vaccinations, treatments for injuries or illnesses, and spay/neuter procedures.

Behavioral Evaluation

Our certified behaviorists assess each dog's temperament, identify trauma-related issues, and develop customized training programs.

Rehabilitation Therapy

Dogs receive specialized care including physical therapy, behavioral modification, socialization training, and emotional support to prepare them for adoption.

Adoption and Rehoming

Our comprehensive adoption process ensures that each dog finds the perfect home. We conduct thorough screenings of potential adopters, provide ongoing support, and maintain follow-up contact to ensure successful transitions.

Application Process

Detailed application and interview process to ensure compatibility between dogs and families.

Meet & Greet

Supervised meetings between potential adopters and dogs to assess compatibility and bonding.

Ongoing Support

Post-adoption support including training resources, behavioral consultation, and emergency assistance.

Our Programs

We offer comprehensive programs designed to address every aspect of dog rescue and rehabilitation, from initial rescue through successful adoption and beyond.

Adoption Program

Our adoption program is designed to create perfect matches between dogs and families. We believe that every successful adoption starts with understanding the needs of both the dog and the adoptive family.

Step 1: Application

Complete our comprehensive adoption application including your living situation, experience with pets, and preferences.

Step 2: Interview

Meet with our adoption counselors to discuss your application and find the best match for your family.

Step 3: Meet & Greet

Spend time with your potential new companion in a comfortable, supervised environment.

Step 4: Home Visit

Our team conducts a friendly home visit to ensure a safe environment for your new pet.

Step 5: Adoption

Complete the adoption paperwork and welcome your new family member home!

Step 6: Follow-up

Receive ongoing support and resources to ensure a successful transition and lifelong bond.

Foster Program

Temporary Homes

Provide loving temporary care for dogs recovering from medical procedures, behavioral training, or awaiting adoption.

Flexible Commitment

Foster periods range from a few weeks to several months, depending on the dog's needs and your availability.

Specialized Foster

Special programs for senior dogs, puppies, dogs with medical needs, and those requiring behavioral rehabilitation.

We provide all necessary supplies, medical care, and ongoing support. Foster families give dogs the individual attention they need to heal and thrive.

Education and Awareness Program

School Visits

Interactive educational programs teaching children about responsible pet ownership, animal welfare, and compassion.

Community Workshops

Free workshops on dog care, training basics, pet safety, and the importance of spaying and neutering.

Digital Outreach

Online resources, social media campaigns, and educational content to reach a broader audience.

Medical and Rehabilitation Program

Veterinary Care

Comprehensive medical care including routine checkups, vaccinations, surgeries, and treatment of injuries and illnesses.

Emergency Services

24/7 emergency medical care for rescued dogs with life-threatening conditions or severe injuries.

Specialized Treatment

Advanced treatments for complex medical conditions, including physical therapy and rehabilitation services.

Community Outreach

Adoption Events

Regular community adoption events, pet fairs, and fundraising activities to connect dogs with potential families.

Partnership Building

Collaborating with local businesses, schools, and organizations to create a network of support for animal welfare.

Advocacy

Working with local government and policy makers to improve animal welfare laws and reduce pet abandonment.
